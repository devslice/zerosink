1. Router Configuration (All Devices)

Configuring your home router automatically protects every device that connects to your home network (TVs, smart plugs, mobile phones, computers) without individual device setup.

  1. Log in to your router's admin panel (typically at 192.168.1.1 or 192.168.0.1).
  2. Navigate to **WAN Settings**, **LAN Settings**, or **DHCP Settings**.
  3. Locate the **DNS Server** configuration section.
  4. Set the Primary DNS Server IP to: .
  5. Save changes and restart your router or re-connect your devices to the Wi-Fi.

2. Windows Configuration

  1. Open **Settings** > **Network & internet** > **Wi-Fi** or **Ethernet**.
  2. Click on the name of your active connection.
  3. Scroll down to **DNS server assignment** and click **Edit**.
  4. Select **Manual**, toggle IPv4 **On**, and enter: .
  5. Save settings. Open command prompt and run ipconfig /flushdns.

3. macOS Configuration

  1. Go to **System Settings** > **Network**.
  2. Select your active connection (Wi-Fi or Ethernet) and click **Details...**
  3. Select the **DNS** tab in the sidebar.
  4. Under DNS Servers, click the **+** button and enter: .
  5. Click **OK** and then **Apply** to save.

Settings apply only when connected to your home Wi-Fi.

  1. Open your device's Wi-Fi Settings and tap the **Info (i)** or gear icon next to your network.
  2. Look for the **DNS** configuration option (often set to Automatic).
